Anderson Letters Markup

This edition of William B. Anderson's Civil War correspondence employs a customized version of the Text Encoding Initiative's (TEI) P5: Guidelines for Electronic Text Encoding and Interchange; this page describes how we applied the TEI Guidelines to the Anderson letters. Complete documentation of our markup is available in a separate document, W. B. Anderson Letters TEI Customization. Formal representations of the markup in .ODD and .RNC format can be found under the "Source Files" menu.

Overview

Composite Text Structure. This edition employs the <teiCorpus> text structure, which provides a <teiHeader> containing descriptive information about the collection of letters and separate <TEI> elements for each letter, each of which contains its own <teiHeader> element providing descriptive information about a particular manuscript letter and a <text> element containing the text of the letter. The <teiHeader> elements for individual letters contain <msDesc> elements that provide physical descriptions of the manuscript sources. For more information about this text structure, see TEI P5, 15.1 Varieties of Composite Text.

Markup of Physical Structure and Page Layout. The manuscript pages, encoded by the empty page break or <pb/> element, are the primary physical structures represented in the markup. Each <pb/> element includes a @facs attribute pointing to a high-resolution scan of that page image. Line breaks are encoded with the empty <lb/> element. No attributes are included in the <lb> element. When envelopes or enclosures accompany letters, they are encoded with <div/> elements containing @type attributes with the values "envelope" or "enclosure," respectively.

Markup of Top-level Textual Structure. The letter constitutes the primary textual structure represented in the markup. It is encoded by a <div> element with the attribute-value pair @type="letter" and an @xml:id attribute with a unique value, e.g., "WBA_18620616_let." The date string (e.g., "18620616" corresponds to the date of the letter as determined by the editors (any discrepancies between that date and the date written in the letter are annotated). Where applicable, the <div> elements enclosing letters, envelopes, and enclosures also contain an @corresp attribute identifying their relationships to one another, thus:

<div type="letter" xml:id="WBA_18621007_let" corresp="#WBA_18621007-env">

Letters typically consist of an opener (<opener>) containing a dateline and salutation (<salute>) followed by a series of paragraphs and a closer. Datelines are encoded with the <dateline> element and contain a <date> element and a @when attribute with the value "YYYY-MM-DD." Where indicated by indentation of a line from the left margin of a page, line breaks part-way across a page, or additional white space between lines, paragraphs are encoded with the <p> element and no attributes. Line breaks throughout are encoded with <lb/> elements. Closing salutations (<salute>) and signatures (<signed>) are contained in a <closer> element. Postscripts are contained in a <postscript> element

Level of Transcription/Encoding. Our transcription follows the manuscript verbatim: spelling, capitalization, and punctuation in the manuscript are followed insofar as they are possible to distinguish. Nonstandard spellings are encoded and reported by a following [sic]; regularized and nonstandard spellings are encoded as follows, but only the manuscript spelling is reported:

<choice><corr>censure</corr><sic>Sensure</sic></choice>

Other manuscript features such as line breaks, recoverable cancellations and interlinear or marginal additions are also encoded. How they are reported depends on the "view" of the manuscript. For example, in the "By MS Page" view, recoverable cancellations and line breaks are reported, as are interlinear additions (indicated with arrows identifying the position of the additions) and marginal additions, indicated with pipes (|). More information about each view can be read by following the "About This View" link at the top of each page in the edition proper.

Other Editorial Apparatus. Links to explanatory annotations appear in all views; links to facsimiles of manuscript pages appear in the "By MS Page" and "Facsimile/Text" views.

Source: These project guidelines are based on The Text Encoding Initiative's Guidelines for Electronic Text Encoding and Interchange (P5).
